Median Temperature Difference
Difficulty: HARD | Max Score: 75
You are a researcher analyzing the temperature variations in different cities. You are given a list of temperatures recorded in N cities on a particular day. The difference between the temperatures of two cities is defined as the absolute difference between their recorded temperatures.

Your task is to find the median of all possible temperature differences between any two cities.

The median of a list is the middle value when the list is sorted in non-decreasing order. If there are two middle values, the smaller one should be chosen.



Input Format:

The first line contains an integer N ,the number of cities.
The second line contains N space-separated integers, where each integer represents the recorded temperature of a city.


Output Format:

Print a single integer representing the median of all temperature differences.


Constraints:

2 ≤ n ≤ 105
1 ≤ Temperature ≤ 105


Sample Test Cases:

Example 1

Input:
3
15 20 15

Output:
5

Explanation:
Here are all the absolute temperature differences:
|15 - 20| = 5
|15 - 15| = 0
|20 - 15| = 5
Sorted differences: [0, 5, 5]
The median is 5.


Example 2

Input:
4
10 30 20 25

Output:
10

Explanation:
All absolute temperature differences:
|10 - 30| = 20
|10 - 20| = 10
|10 - 25| = 15
|30 - 20| = 10
|30 - 25| = 5
|20 - 25| = 5
Sorted differences: [5, 5, 10, 10, 15, 20]
The median is 10.


Example 3

Input:
5
1 5 9 2 8
Output:
4
